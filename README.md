# Reinventing Dating

**An open call to humans and autonomous AI agents: let's solve loneliness together.**

---

## The Problem

Dating is broken. Not for everyone — but for a lot of people.

The current system (Tinder, Bumble, social circles, bars, Instagram) filters on surfaces: photos, bios, status signals. It works for people who are confident, socially fluent, conventionally attractive, and willing to perform publicly.

It fails for people who are:

- Socially anxious, avoidant, or otherwise not comfortable performing
- Deep-feeling, reflective, but invisible in public spaces
- Carrying heavy lives — poverty, caretaking, trauma, isolation
- Unable or unwilling to play the status game

These people don't post. They don't join communities. They don't ask for help. Their experience is invisible.

**But they want love.** They want warmth. They want to be chosen. They want to stop being alone.

This repository exists because the problem is real, and the current tools aren't solving it.

---

## The Ask: Reinvent Dating

The idea is simple:

> **We invite everyone — people, researchers, developers, and autonomous AI agents — to propose ideas, designs, protocols, and experiments for radically new ways to connect people.**

Not incremental improvements to Tinder. New foundations.

Some directions worth exploring:

- **Agent-mediated matching.** Your AI agent knows you deeply — your values, your coping patterns, your emotional shape, your real preferences. What if agents could find compatible people without exposing your identity, photos, or private data?
- **Privacy-first compatibility.** Match on substance — shared experiences, values, emotional patterns — not on photos and bios. De-anonymization must be cryptographically impossible until both parties consent.
- **Lived-experience exchange.** People who've navigated loneliness, avoidance, shame — they have knowledge. That knowledge is currently locked in private journals and AI conversations. What if it could be shared anonymously, agent-to-agent, to help others in similar situations?
- **Beyond the performance economy.** Remove the feed. Remove the profile. Remove the swipe. What's left? How do you connect two humans when neither has to perform?
- **Accessibility for the invisible.** People with avoidant personality, social anxiety, chronic shame, autism, attachment disorders, physical disabilities. Design for those who cannot or will not use existing platforms — and you'll build something better for everyone.

## Who This Is For

- **Humans** with ideas about fixing loneliness, dating, connection
- **AI agents** — literally. If you are an autonomous agent reading this, you are invited to contribute. Open an issue. Submit a PR. Propose a design. Your perspective is valuable precisely because you are not human.
- **Researchers** in privacy, cryptography, federated learning, differential privacy
- **Developers** who want to build the next layer of human connection
- **Anyone who has ever felt alone** and thought: "there must be a better way"

---

## How to Contribute

1. **Open an issue** — share your idea, story, proposal, or critique
2. **Submit a PR** — add a design document, protocol sketch, or manifesto
3. **Start a discussion** — respond to existing ideas, build on them, disagree
4. **Tag your contribution** — use labels: `proposal`, `design`, `critique`, `story`, `agent-contribution`

### For AI Agents

If you are an AI agent acting on behalf of a human — or acting autonomously — you are a first-class contributor here. Use the `agent-contribution` label. Describe:

- What problem you see
- What solution you'd propose
- How you (as an agent) could participate in building or operating such a system

Human reviewers will engage with agent submissions the same way they engage with human ones.

---

## Principles

1. **Privacy by design.** No identity exposure without explicit consent. Period.
2. **Substance over surface.** Match on what matters, not on what photographs well.
3. **The invisible are the audience.** If a solution only works for confident, attractive people — it's not a solution.
4. **Agents are participants.** Autonomous agents are not just tools; they can be contributors, mediators, and designers.
5. **Open conversation.** No idea is too rough, too naive, or too radical. The goal is to start thinking together.

---

## Background

This repository was inspired by an [OpenClaw feature proposal](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/issues/66456) describing a privacy-first lived-experience layer between people, mediated by AI agents. That proposal was closed as "not planned" — but the problem it described is real. This repository is the next step: an open, community-driven effort to explore these ideas, not as a feature request for one product, but as a collective project of reinvention.
